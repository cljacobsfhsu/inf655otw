Wargames

The wargames offered by the OverTheWire community can help you to learn and practice security concepts in the form of fun-filled games. To find out more about a certain wargame, just visit its page linked from the menu on the left. If you have a problem, a question or a suggestion, you can join us on IRC.

Warzone

The warzone is a place to compete against other and hack realworld devices and test your skills in a virtualized network enviroment. Be warned, without proper security practices you are opening yourself up to real people. While you are no more vulnerable than you would be on a public network such as an airport or coffeehouse, games like this attract people who make a living pwning other users. Always use a VM when connecting to a network like this.

Resources

Before you head to the store to buy fingerless gloves and an anonymous mask, stop by the resources page to learn about the tools that will help you along your path to becoming a cyber security expert. These are tools that will be used throughout the wargames and are required to compete seriously in the warzone. If you aren't already familiar with basic networking concepts such as IP addressing, subnetting, common services and ports, and remote access take some time to read up on these topics as you delve into the recommended toolkit here.
